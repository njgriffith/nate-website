01-14-2025: biking places and cold weather

Sometimes, I like to bike places if I can. The roads around where I live aren't super safe for it, but if there's a decently car free or trail-able way to get there I like to give it a shot. I also like going for runs, rides, or just to walk around in the cold, or the rain, or the cold and rain. Other people tell me that rainy weather drains them of energy but I've always felt the opposite. I have my most energy when the weather cold, dreary, or rainy. Thats not to say I don't appreciate a hot summer day, or just a warm spring day in the sun with a breeze, but something about these kinds of days scratch a certain itch I have.

I also don't know if part of me, because my life is pretty easy, want's something strenuous to trudge through to make it feel like I actually "worked for my meal". Like if I walk somewhere for 30 mins in the cold rain, once I get inside I feel like I activate the same part of my brain as a caveman who finally got to sit down next to his cave fire after a long day of fighting tigers. And because that mart of my brain never gets it's exercise, I feel like it enjoys coming out of the box every now and then.

I remember one time I had to get star anise and a couple other things for a recipe on a weekend day. I knew there was supposed to be a tropical storm coming through but I decided to go anyways figuring I'd be hit with some rain, no big deal. Well...during that 16 mile bike ride I got pelted...utterly soaked and damn near blown to a halt at times. But when I got home and threw my sopping wet clothes in the bath and got clean and dry, I felt like it was of the best, most memorable bike rides I'd ever been on. Now admittedly this was totally stupid. Like 3 large pine trees in my path fell over during the ride. It wasn't a fun little drizzle anymore it was a read deal storm.

And part of me thinks that this is just how I enjoy nature. It feels real, and because of that it feels immersive, it feels special. If I only ever went outside on a sunny 70 degree refreshing gust of wind lemonade drinking ice cream eating "canonbaalllllll" ass day, it would be inauthentic, contrived. I like to feed my caveman lizard brain. I went on a hike in Oregon in September up and around Mt. Hood. Me and my brother walked all day every day, and when we would get to a good looking spot to pitch the tent by some water, I'd drop my stuff and sit down barefoot by the river and eat sardines out of a tin. This made caveman Nate feel very happy.
