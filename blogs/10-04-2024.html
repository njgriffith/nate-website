Now I know it's probably kinda cringe to have a little paragraph about vaulting and then a much larger paragraph about a video game, but ummmmm…. uhhhhhh…
Celeste is a very difficult and precise platformer, and I'm gonna give you some introduction to set the stage for this challenge I'm working on. So the main story of Celeste has 7 levels of increasing difficulty, and they're honestly not too bad. It'll be a struggle but pretty much anyone could get through these eventually. I think I spent about 8 hours of play time on them. Upon completing the story there's an additional level 8 that gets a bit harder than the final level of the main story. But here's where things get interesting. Each level has a hidden casette tape that, if you find it, unlocks a "B-side" for that level. B sides are usually slightly shorter, but have a huuuge increase in difficulty. Manage to beat all of those, and you unlock "C-sides". C sides are very short, only having 3 rooms to complete, but they will be the most difficult screens in the game. So now that you understand the difficulty curve of the game and its 24 levels (8 a-sides, b-sides, and c-sides), I can explain the 202 strawberry challenge.

There are 202 strawberries you can collect in the game. 175 are simple, just hidden somewhere in the a-sides, but usually not too hard to get, som I'm done with those. So that leaves 27, and these are the ones worth talking about. Once you've finish all the c-sides, a golden strawberry will appear at the start of each level. If you pick it up and finish the entire level without dying, congrats! You got a golden strawberry. Easier said than done of course since some of these levels regularly have people dying close to 1000 times before their first clear. So do that once for every a, b, and c-side, and you're at 199/202.

So what are the last 3 then? First one is pretty straightforward, beat the first level without dashing. This however requires a lot of pixel perfect jumps since you can use the most important mechanic in the game. Now, to introduce the last 2 berries, I have to talk about the actual final chapter, Farewell.

Chapter 9, or "Farewell", is far and away the longest and most difficult map in the game. It's actually not even close. There's no b or c side, just chapter 9. It features screens that regularly are at and even exceeding the difficulty of the hardest c side screens. But instead of 3 rooms, it's 93 *thunder crashing* There's one hidden berry where you complete the entire final screen of Farewell (which is by far the longest screen) and at the very end instead of finishing it, you have to traverse backwards and to a secret room that's about as hard as they get in the game. Complete that and the moon berry is yours. And of course, the final berry, strawberry #202... the Farewell golden. If you somehow get to this point, you have a task before you that will make all your previous achievements feel vanishingly insignificant in comparison. Complete all of Farewell without dying once, and the last berry is yours. I died over 3000 times completing Farewell the first time, so if I get there it'll be a while before I can shave that number down. Here's a table of the challenge berries I have so far:

The worst movie of all time

This movie has completely changes my brain, in fact it's probably still happening. I have no idea what I witnessed the other night, and can't make sense of it. Like you might say a movie is bad cause it's boring but I couldn't take my eyes off the screen for a second. I pissed in the seat!
